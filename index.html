Michigan's Capital Lansing & East Lansing

By the Editorial Staff  ·  Updated April 2026

Michigan's state capital sits at the confluence of the Grand and Cedar rivers, a mid-sized city with a working-class industrial past, a lively university town next door in East Lansing, and more going on than most visitors expect.

Home to Michigan State University, the R.E. Olds Transportation Museum, and a downtown that's been quietly reinventing itself for a decade, Lansing rewards the curious traveller willing to look past its reputation as a city you just pass through.

[ Map: Lansing, MI region ]
Lansing sits in Ingham County, roughly equidistant from Detroit, Grand Rapids, and the Straits of Mackinac.

Getting There

Capital Region International Airport (LAN) serves Lansing with connections through Chicago O'Hare and Detroit Metro. Amtrak's Pere Marquette line connects to Chicago, and the Blue Water line passes through on its way to Port Huron. By road, I-96 and I-69 intersect here, making Lansing an easy drive from most of lower Michigan.

What to See

The Michigan State Capitol is the obvious anchor — the dome was restored in the 1990s and the interior is genuinely spectacular, open for free self-guided tours most weekdays. A short walk takes you to the R.E. Olds Transportation Museum, which charts Lansing's history as an early hub of American auto manufacturing — Ransom Olds built his first car here before the Oldsmobile name moved to Detroit.

East Lansing bleeds into Lansing's eastern edge; the MSU campus is vast and worth wandering, with the Broad Art Museum (Zaha Hadid–designed, free admission) as a particular highlight.

Food & Drink

The Lansing food scene punches above its weight. HopCat on Michigan Avenue is a reliable craft beer stop; El Oasis on Cedar Street is a local institution for Mexican food. The LSJ's annual "Best of Lansing" consistently surfaces neighbourhood gems that don't make it onto national listicles — worth checking before your visit.

East Lansing's Grove Street corridor is the place for a longer evening, with a cluster of bars and restaurants aimed at the MSU crowd but perfectly enjoyable for everyone else.

Quick Facts

  • Population: ~112,000 (city)
  • County: Ingham
  • State capital since: 1847
  • Nearest major airport: LAN (in-city)
  • Time zone: ET (UTC−5/−4)
